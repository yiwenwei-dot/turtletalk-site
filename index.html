Meet Tammy

A patient listener who never runs out of warmth.

Tammy is the tiny sea turtle at the heart of TurtleTalk. When your child has something big to say, Tammy listens — really listens — and replies in a warm, kind voice.

No scripts. No screens. No judgment. Just a safe place for kids to talk through their feelings and come out feeling a little braver.

How It Works

Three gentle steps, every day.

TurtleTalk turns everyday moments into opportunities for connection. Here's how a typical day with Tammy unfolds.

01
💬

Talk

Your child taps the turtle, holds a conversation with Tammy about their day, their friends, their worries — anything on their mind.

02
🌱

Reflect

Tammy helps reframe hard moments with gentle questions and stories, turning big feelings into small brave missions for the week.

03
🌟

Grow

At dinner, parents get a soft prompt: "Ask about Mia today." A simple conversation starter that makes the whole family closer.

What's Inside

Built for kids. Designed with parents.

Every feature was shaped by interviews with parents, educators, and kids themselves — to make sure TurtleTalk is something you feel good about handing to your child.

For Kids

A friend who always has time.

  • Warm voice replies — Tammy speaks with a gentle, child-friendly voice, not a robot.
  • Brave missions — small challenges like "say hi to someone new" to build courage, one step at a time.
  • No scary screens — conversation-first, no flashing graphics or addictive loops.
  • Kid-safe responses — every reply is shaped to be age-appropriate and emotionally gentle.
For Parents

A bridge back to the dinner table.

  • Weekly reports — a gentle summary of what your child talked about and how they're feeling.
  • Conversation starters — soft prompts that help you check in without interrogating.
  • Book & movie recommendations — Tammy suggests age-appropriate stories that echo what your child is feeling, making education simple and accessible for every family.
  • Transparent by design — no black-box AI tutor; you can see what Tammy says and why.
